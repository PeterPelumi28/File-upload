THE IMPACT OF SOCIAL MEDIA ON ACADEMIC PERFORMANCE IN NIGERIA

Author: Adegbite Peter O. | Date: 31st November, 2024

Introduction

In recent years, social media has emerged as a dominant force in the lives of young people worldwide, particularly in Nigeria. With the proliferation of smartphones and internet access, platforms such as Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and WhatsApp have transformed communication and information sharing among students. As these digital spaces become increasingly integrated into daily routines, their potential impact on academic performance is a growing concern. Social media offers numerous benefits, including access to vast educational resources, collaborative learning opportunities, and the ability to connect with peers for academic support. However, it also presents challenges, such as distractions, procrastination, and a decline in face-to-face interactions. Understanding the dual nature of social media is crucial for educators, parents, and students alike.

Objectives of The Report

The primary objectives of this study are:

  • To analyze the extent of social media usage among students in Nigeria.
  • To evaluate the perceived impacts of social media on students' study habits and academic performance.
  • To identify the positive and negative effects of social media on academic outcomes.

Definition of Key Terms

  • Social Media: Digital platforms that facilitate social interaction and content sharing among users, such as Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, and WhatsApp.
  • Academic Performance: The extent to which a student meets their educational goals, often measured through grades, GPA, and exam results.
  • Study Habits: The behaviors and practices that students engage in to facilitate learning and academic success, including time management and study techniques.
